Room Away from Home

Overview

Throughout this project, I created a mobile application prototype that demonstrates a logical user interface. This application is based on a simulated database. As a team of three, we decided on a certain user demographic and researched their needs during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Objective

To create an application that allows people to isolate themselves in a designated place safely during COVID. It would also help the government reduce the spread of COVID-19, avoid overloading hospitals, and track individuals who are doing their self-isolation.

Process

To do the research, I used Google Forms to conduct UX surveys, which collected demographic information and asked close-ended questions related to participants' experience with COVID-19 quarantine/isolation. Then the respondents' needs were analyzed through the quantitative data collected.

Based on the user personas and the data collected, together as a group, we designed the basic layout and the low-fidelity wireframes.

Different iterations of medium to high-fidelity wireframes and their style guides were created through Figma. The combination of soft complementary colours, the san serif choice of font, and the round corners of my first iteration make the application more friendly and aesthetically pleasing.

My focused pages were the home screen and the search pages — map page, calendar, and search results. After adding images to the prototype, I decided to use darker complementary colours which match the images better.

Reflection

At the beginning of the project, I spent a lot of time trying to decide on a colour scheme that would match the first layouts. However, as I was adding more features, the colours had to change again to match the new iterations. I realized the amount of time I spent choosing the colours could have been spent perfecting the interface. For future prototyping projects, I would try to focus more on making the interaction features more smooth feeling.
